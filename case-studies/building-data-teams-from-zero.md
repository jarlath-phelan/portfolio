# Case Study: Building Data Teams from Zero

## Subtitle
What it takes to be the first analytics hire and build a data function at a growing company — twice

## Context

Two separate experiences building data teams from scratch at growing companies:

1. **A luxury flash-sales startup** (UK, ~2013–2017): Joined as the first analytics hire. Built the data function from zero to a team of 4 over 4 years, eventually becoming Head of Data Science.

2. **A Japanese marketplace expanding to Europe** (~2017–2018): Hired specifically to build out the data team for the European expansion. Senior Data Science Manager role.

## The First-Hire Playbook

### Phase 1: Prove Value Fast
- When you're the first data person, nobody knows what "data science" means in practice
- First priority: find the biggest business question that has no data answer and deliver one
- At the flash-sales startup: TV advertising attribution was the first big win — nobody knew which TV spots drove sales

### Phase 2: Build the Infrastructure
- No data warehouse, no pipelines, no dashboards — you build it all
- Chose tools ahead of the curve (early Looker adoption in the UK, among the first European customers)
- Established the data pipeline architecture that the company ran on for years

### Phase 3: Hire and Grow the Team
- **Flash-sales startup:** Built from 1 to 4 (Data Scientist, Data Engineer, CRM Manager, CRM Executive)
- **Japanese marketplace:** Hired specifically to build the European data team, managed data scientist directly, collaborated with C-suite on KPI definition
- Key lesson: the first hire after you sets the culture. Choose carefully.

## Impact — Flash-Sales Startup (4 years)

### TV Attribution Model
- Built an 8-minute dynamic attribution window linking TV spots to website traffic spikes
- 100% improvement in ROI measurement accuracy
- Identified £300K in directly attributable sales
- Result: TV advertising budget was optimized, not cut — data showed it worked

### Content Ranking System
- Built personalized content ranking with holdout control groups
- 5% lift in sales from personalized product placement
- Controlled experiment design in a startup context (no experimentation platform — built the measurement from scratch)

### CRM Experimentation
- Referral program: 2x growth through data-driven optimization
- Email marketing: +10% open rate, +5% click-through, +2% sales per email
- Built the measurement frameworks that proved these results

## Impact — Japanese Marketplace

### Business KPIs
- Worked with CEO, CFO, and product leadership to define the company's core KPIs
- Migrated BI tooling from Chartio to Looker (another early adoption)
- Managed feasibility assessment for AI pricing from photos (brought in external consultants, ran rigorous evaluation, concluded insufficient signal — saved the company from a bad investment)

## Techniques Used

Attribution modeling (dynamic time-window), holdout control groups, A/B testing in startup contexts, cohort analysis, BI platform selection and migration, team building and hiring

## Lessons Learned

- The first data hire at a company is 70% infrastructure and 30% analysis — you have to build before you can analyze
- Early tool choices compound: picking the right BI platform (Looker) saved years of technical debt
- In startups, you need to demonstrate value in the first 2–4 weeks or you'll lose organizational buy-in
- Building a data team is not just hiring — it's creating the culture, standards, and processes that make the team effective
- "I grow over years, not months" — tenure at both companies (4 years, 1.5 years for a short expansion project) shows commitment
